Maratha Culture

Arts and Literature

The Maratha period saw a flourishing of literature in Marathi, with works like Dasbodh by Samarth Ramdas. Poetry, especially powadas (ballads) celebrating Maratha victories, gained popularity.

Architecture

Maratha architecture is known for its hill forts, such as Raigad and Pratapgad, showcasing military ingenuity and aesthetic beauty. Temples built during this era often featured a blend of North and South Indian styles.

Customs and Traditions

Marathas celebrated festivals like Gudi Padwa with great enthusiasm. The traditional attire included the nine-yard saree for women and the dhoti-kurta for men.
