British Colonial Period Overview

East India Company

The British East India Company laid the foundation for colonial rule in India.

Sepoy Mutiny of 1857

A significant uprising against British rule, also known as India's First War of Independence.

The British Raj

Direct rule by the British Crown from 1858 to 1947, reshaping India's political landscape.

Indian Railways

The introduction of railways in 1853, a lasting legacy of British colonial infrastructure.

Indian National Congress

Founded in 1885, it became a major force in the Indian independence movement.

Partition of India

The division of British India into India and Pakistan in 1947, marking the end of colonial rule.

Culture During British Colonial Period

Education and Language

The British introduced English education, leading to the emergence of a new educated class. English became the language of administration and higher education.

Architecture

Indo-Saracenic architecture blended Indian, Islamic, and European styles, seen in buildings like the Victoria Memorial in Calcutta.

Social Reforms

British rule saw the abolition of practices like sati and child marriage, as well as the promotion of widow remarriage.

Gallery

Queen Victoria

Proclaimed Empress of India in 1876, symbolizing the height of British imperial power.

Taj Mahal

An iconic monument that fascinated the British, leading to efforts in its preservation.

A lasting legacy of British colonial infrastructure, connecting the vast subcontinent.
