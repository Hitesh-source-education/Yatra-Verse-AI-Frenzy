Timeline of the Mughal Empire

1526

Babur defeats Ibrahim Lodi at the First Battle of Panipat, establishing the Mughal Empire

1556

Akbar ascends to the throne, beginning a golden age of the Mughal Empire

1605

Jahangir becomes emperor, known for his love of art and nature

1631

Shah Jahan begins construction of the Taj Mahal

1658

Aurangzeb becomes emperor after a war of succession

1707

Death of Aurangzeb, marking the beginning of the decline of the Mughal Empire

1739

Nadir Shah's invasion weakens Mughal power significantly

1857

The last Mughal emperor, Bahadur Shah Zafar, is deposed by the British, marking the end of the Mughal Empire

Culture During the Mughal Empire

Art and Architecture

The Mughals were great patrons of art and architecture. They developed a unique Indo-Islamic architectural style, exemplified by monuments like the Taj Mahal, Fatehpur Sikri, and the Red Fort.

Literature and Language

Persian was the official language of the Mughal court. The period saw a flourishing of literature in Persian, as well as the development of Urdu as a language. The Mughal era also saw translations of Sanskrit texts into Persian.

Music and Painting

Mughal emperors patronized music and developed new forms like Khayal. Mughal miniature paintings, known for their intricate details and vibrant colors, reached their zenith during this period.
